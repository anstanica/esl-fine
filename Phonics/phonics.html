Phonics | Lesson Plan

Phonics is a method for teaching people how to read and write an alphabetic language. It is done by demonstrating the relationship between the sounds of the spoken language, and the letters or groups of letters or syllables of the written language.
For learners phonics is the ability to identify letters and sounds and to use them to decode words.
Teaching phonics should be the core of your teaching course, So, we advise you to start teaching phonics Once the students have a grasp on the basics.


Remember we use TEFL methodology to teach phonics to students

Presentation, Practice and Production.

Presentation : where the teacher introduces the topic to the students.
Practice : where the teacher gives the students exercises to practice the topic.
Production : where the teacher gives the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation.

Introduction

introduce the letters of the alphabet in the order they appear in the alphabet. Use a physical alphabet chart to show the letters and have the students point to them as they say the letter name. After that, introduce simple phonics words such as cat, dog, and rat.
Next: introduce the different letter sounds and how they combine to form words. It's important to focus on short vowel sounds first, as they are the most common and are easier for kids to hear. Make sure to practice each sound multiple times, as this will help kids to internalize it. Additionally, you can use fun activities and games to reinforce the letter-sound connections. As kids become more comfortable with letter-sound combinations, you can move on to two-letter blends, such as "sh" and "th". Again, practice each blend multiple times and use engaging activities to help kids make the connection between the sound and the letters. Finally, move on to long vowel sounds, such as "igh" and "eigh". This can be more challenging for kids, so it's important to take your time and provide plenty of practice. Additionally, make sure to review all of the letter-sound combinations periodically to ensure your students are retaining the information.

Practice

Use simple examples:
Provide simple examples of phonics in a sentence. This will help the students understand how to use phonics in a sentence. for example:
I run.
I am happy.
Use the flashcards . Next show them Flashcards and ask them to repeat after you several times.
Then use play pass the flashcards. The students will pass the flashcards to each other and say the Verb.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what color is this?
    Student B: It's red.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

  • In-Class Games

    • Sound Matching

      This game is all about matching the right sound to the right letter. Write out letters on small cards and then have your the students match them with the sound they make. You can also use objects, pictures or phrases to represent the sound.

    • CVC Reveal


      For this game you need 2 CVC flip books, You can buy them online or you can make them yourself.
      To play the game

    • 1 - Give each student a flip book.
      2 - They stand back to back. and choose a random word from the book.
      3 - The teacher counts 1-3 while counting the students move three steps forward, then the teacher says turn or reveal.
      4 - the students face each other and read the word from the other's flip book as quick as possible.
      5 - The first one reads the word wins.

    • Phonics Bingo

      One person flips an alphabet card and calls out The letter or the sound if makes. Players cover that letter with a counter. The first +0 cover all squares calls out 'bingo'!
