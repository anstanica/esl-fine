Shapes | Lesson Plan

Shapes are everywhere around us. Shapes are 2 sided shapes like digons and 3 sided shapes like pyramids, cones, and triangles . Shapes are also 4 sided shapes like rectangles, squares, and 5 sided shapes like pentagons. 6 sided shapes like hexagons, 7 sided shapes like heptagons, 8 sided shapes like octagons or no sides at all like circles and ovals. And the list goes on. You will teach the shapes according to your students level. This Lesson plan will help you to teach the shapes to your students.

How to teach a song

Introduction

1 - Start by introducing the shapes using pictures or visuals.
2- Draw the shapes on the board or show pictures of objects that are shaped like the shape you are teaching.

Questions:
- What is the shape of the sun?
- What shape is it ?
- How many sides does it have?

Practice

Have the students practice saying the names of the shapes out loud. Make sure to provide positive feedback for their efforts.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity
    above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

Below are some activities that you can use in this lesson:

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • What is the shape?



      - Click on the shape that matches the description.

    • Shapes Angry BirdS



      - Click the correct shapes to advance to the next level.

    • How Many shapes?



      - Count the objects that matches the shape.

  • In-Class Games

    • “I Spy”

      Have the students stand up and walk around the classroom. Tell them to look for a shape. When they find it, they should say “I spy with my little eye, something that is a circle.” Then they should sit down.

    • Draw It

      Have the students draw their own shapes. Give them paper and markers and have them draw shapes and write the names of the shapes next to them.

    • Shape Sorting

      - Give the kids a pile of cards with a variety of shapes and ask them to sort them into the correct shape piles.
      - Or you can draw the shapes on the floor, then collect some objects from around the classroom and ask the students to sort them into the correct shape piles.

  • Online Games.

    • Labelled Diagram

      - Drag and drop pins to their correct places.

    • Random Wheel

Wrap Up

End the lesson with a fun activity like a shape scavenger hunt. Place shapes around the classroom and have students find them and match them to the shapes you’ve taught.
