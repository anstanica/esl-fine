Colors| Lesson Plan

Colors are a part of every other topic in the curriculum. We teach colors to our students so that they can recognize them and use them in their daily lives. Which color is your favorite? What color is your shirt? What color is your hair? What color is this / that ?.
Remember we use TEFL methodology to teach colors to students

Presentation, Practice and Production.

Presentation : where the teacher introduces the topic to the students.
Practice : where the teacher gives the students exercises to practice the topic.
Production : where the teacher gives the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation.

Introduction

Most of the time the majority of the students know the colors, but we still need to introduce them to the colors in English. We can do this by showing them the flashcards below of different colors and asking them to name the colors.

Example:
Teacher: What color is this?
Student: It's red.

Or we can connect the colors to a previous lesson. For example, if we taught the students the names of the fruits and vegetables, we can ask them to name the colors of the fruits and vegetables.

Click on the flashcards to preview before download.



Practice

To practice the colors, we can use the flashcards that we just downloaded. Next show them Flashcards and ask them to repeat after you several times.
Then use the isolation practice , where students play pass the flashcards. The students will pass the flashcards to each other and say the color.
The practice here is divided into three sections:

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what color is this?
    Student B: It's red.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

You can apply the practice in any order you want.
But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Colors game



      choose the correct word that matches the color.

    • Word Search

       

      Find out the colors corresponding to the paint drops within the time limit (1 minute).

  • In-Class Games

    • Roll the dice

      Roll the dice and say the color.
      You will need a die and colors flashcards, and numbers from 1 to 6.
      Lay the flashcards on the table 6 colors in a row and 6 numbers in order from 1 to 6.
      The students will roll the dice and say the color that matches the number.
      For example, if the student rolls the number 3, he will say the color that is in the third column.

    • Dirty Monkey

      A - Make two students sit on the floor in front of each other leaving enough space between them to place three flashcards.
      B - Place the three flashcards between them and give them time to think about the words they have in front of them.
      C - Once they look ready, loudly say the name on one flashcard. The first one to touch it wins and stays there and the loser goes to sit down.
      D - Give one star each; as the winner stays there he can get as many stars as he can as long as he keeps on winning.

    • Flashcard Race

      Set all the flashcards on the whiteboard like a board game with a start and a finish line. Make then throw a dice and reproduce the vocab. they land on. put some ‘Return to Start’, ‘Go back two’, ‘Advance two’, ‘Star’, and any other ‘event squares’ that you may think of to make the game more interesting.

  • Online Games.

    • Concentration



      The aim is match words and images by revealing corresponding pairs.
      The items are hidden behind squares and are revealed when clicked.
      To match all the pairs, you have to concentrate and remember where the items are.

    • Hangman



      - This is a version Of the classic game Of hangman in Which you try to guess the word or       phrase before the man dies.
      - TO play you click on the letters to try and reveal the word.
      - If the word contains the letter, then it is revealed, but if the word doesn't contain the letter then a part of the hangman picture is drawn.
      - When the picture is completely drawn, the man is hung and the game ends.

Wrap up / Review

You can wrap up the lesson by playing a game and by doing a review.
For example, you can play a game of : “I spy” .
Teacher : I spy with my little eye something that is red .
The students will have to guess what the teacher is talking about, and they will run around the classroom looking for something red.
