Toys | Lesson Plan

In this lesson, students will learn about toys. They will learn the names of toys and play a game to practice the names of toys.

Introduction

Start by having the students brainstorm the different types of toys they know. This will help them practice their vocabulary and get them thinking about the topic. After the brainstorming activity, you can introduce the different types of toys and have the students sort them into different categories. For example, they can sort toys into categories such as outdoor toys, musical toys, educational toys, etc Download the flashcards to practice the names of the numbers on the clock face and the names of the hour hand and the minute hand.

Practice

- Make sure to start with basic words such as ball, car, doll, and truck. Explain what each of these words mean and show a few examples of each item. - Use flashcards to help kids remember the words.
Use the toys to create "Toys Show" for the kids to watch. change your voice to make it more interesting.
Create a dialogue between the toys and the kids.
Example:
Teddy : Hello, I am Teddy. I am a soft toy. I am a bear. I am a friend. I am a toy. I am a teddy bear.
Doll: Hello, I am a doll. my name is 'Dolly', I can dance and sing. Car : I am a car, I go vroom vroom.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what is this?
    Student B: It's a pencil.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

You can apply the practice in any order you want.
But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

Below are some activities for practicing this lesson:

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Guess the toy



      Guess the missing word.

    • Puzzle



      Click the puzzle to reveal the hidden toy.

  • In-Class Games

    • Role play Toys

      You can have the students practice speaking by having them roleplay as different types of toys. This will help them practice their pronunciation and speaking skills. For EXample:

      Student A: I am a toy car. I go vroom vroom.
      Student B: I am a teddy bear. I am soft and cuddly.
      Student C: I am a toy train. I go choo choo.

    • Toys Story

      Set a race between the toys. You can have the students Make a list of toys and have kids match the name of the toy to descriptions of what they do.

    • Toy Scavenger Hunt

      Hide toys around the room and have kids search for them. Every time they find one, they must describe it using a sentence in English.

    • Toy Charades

      Pick a toy and have kids act out what the toy does without using words.

    • Toy Writing

      Have kids write a story using the toys they have. They can create a fun story by having the toys interact with each other.

      Here is an example
      Once upon a time, there was a small stuffed animal, named Teddy. Teddy was a friendly, green bear with a bright red nose and a big, friendly smile. He lived in a cozy little toy box in the playroom of a family of four. Teddy loved when the family visited the playroom and played together. He especially enjoyed when the youngest of the family, a toddler, came in to play. The toddler was always so full of joy and love, and Teddy really enjoyed being around that energy. Every time the toddler came to play, Teddy got to join in on the fun. He loved to be thrown up in the air, chased around the room, or taken on an adventure. He was always so excited to do something fun with the toddler. At the end of the day, when the family was ready to leave, Teddy was always sad to see them go. He would lay quietly in the toy box until he could hear the toddler's laughter again. Teddy was a beloved toy, and the toddler always looked forward to spending time with him.

  • Online Games.

    • Match Pairs

      Match the word to the picture.

Wrap up / Review

To wrap up the lesson, you could have the students draw a picture of their favorite toy or write a short story about it. This will help them practice their writing skills and give them a chance to be creative.
