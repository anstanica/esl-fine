Seasons | Lesson Plan

Depending on the age of the kids, there are different activities that can help them learn English while learning about the different seasons. For younger kids, you may want to start with some basic vocabulary, like words for the four seasons, trees and plants that grow in different seasons, and weather conditions associated with each season. For older kids, you could introduce more complex topics like different holidays associated with each season or common activities that people do in each season. You could also use games and activities, like finding objects in a nature scavenger hunt, or reading books about the different seasons. No matter what activities you choose, the most important thing is to make it fun and engaging so the kids stay engaged and can learn more.

Introduction

- The seasons are the periods of the year that are marked by changes in weather, temperature, and the amount of daylight.
With that in mind, it is important to link the seasons to the weather.
So, first review the weather vocabulary with your students. Ask your students about the weather these days.
Let's say their answer is "it's hot ", then you can tell them because it's summer. It's always hot in summer.
Then you can continue with the other seasons.

Questions:
- What season is it?
- what is the next season?
- What is your favorite season?

  • In-Class Games

    • Word Association

      Have the students sit in a circle and say a season-related word, like "snow." Then, the next student has to say a word related to the previous one, such as "snowman." This game can be played until everyone in the circle has had a turn. - More words to help :
      - snowman - cold - ice - snow - rain - umbrella - leaves - trees - flowers - sun - rain - raincoat.

    • Seasons Activities

      These are activities that you can do with your students to help them learn about the seasons.

      Spring: Have your students create a flower collage with different colored paper or construction paper. You can also have them draw a picture of a blooming tree or garden with lots of flowers.
      Summer: Have them draw a beach scene with sand, waves, and beach umbrellas, or a picture of a colorful garden of flowers.
      Autumn: Have your students make a leaf collage with leaves of different shapes and colors. Have them draw a picture of a pumpkin patch or an autumn landscape with a bright orange sky. You can also have them play a game of apple bobbing or a game of apple toss.
      Winter: Have your students make a snowman out of snow or cotton balls. Have them draw a picture of a sledding hill with snowflakes falling from the sky. You can also have them make snow globes out of jars, glitter, and water or a winter-themed diorama with a snow-covered house and trees.

    • Out-door Activities

      How to play ?
      - Snowball Toss: This game is perfect for winter! All you need is a bag of snowballs (or make your own) and two lines of people. Take turns tossing the snowballs to each other and see how far you can go.
      - Summer Scavenger Hunt: Summertime is the perfect time for a scavenger hunt! Prepare a list of items related to the season (think summer fruits, flowers, and insects) and have the kids search for the items.
      - Spring Flower Garden: This game requires a bit of prep work, but it’s worth it! Create a flower garden out of cardboard boxes and paper flowers. Each flower can represent a point and the player with the most points at the end wins!
      - Fall Leaf Hunt: Gather a bunch of fall leaves and have the kids search for the different colors and shapes. You can also have them collect a certain number of leaves or make a leaf mosaic together.
