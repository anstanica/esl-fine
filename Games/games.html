House

Games | Lesson Plan

Objective: The objective of this lesson is for the students to learn and practice language skills in a fun and engaging way.

Materials:

  • Games (board games, card games, etc.)
  • Flashcards
  • Whiteboard
  • Markers

Procedure:

  1. Introduce the topic of games and explain why it can be a useful and fun way to learn language skills.
  2. Divide the class into pairs or small groups.
  3. Provide each group with a game, flashcards and a whiteboard.
  4. Explain the game and the rules to the students.
  5. Ask the students to create their own rules for the game and write them on the whiteboard.
  6. Have the students take turns playing the game and using the flashcards to practice language skills.
  7. Monitor and assist the students as they play.
  8. After the game is finished, review the language skills they practiced and ask them to give feedback on the game.
  9. Encourage the students to come up with their own ideas for games and activities.
  10. Wrap up the lesson by summarizing the main points.

Assessment: At the end of the lesson, you can assess the students' understanding of the language skills by asking them to explain the game and the rules they created. You can also ask them to write their own game or activity and explain it to the class.

Practice

We always fascinated by how much the students love to /zoo-animals/, so why not use this to your advantage?, Not all of us are artists, but we can all draw a simple house.
- Start by drawing a simple square on the board.
- Then draw a triangle on top of the square.
- Then draw lines to make the windows and doors.
- then draw walls inside the square.
- continue drawing the rest of the house.
Point to each part of the house and ask the students to repeat after you, 2 or 3 times.
- Then ask the students to draw a house on their own and color it.

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Parts of the house



      A ppt game to practice the parts of the house.

    • Family At Home



      - Multiple choice game to practice family members, and the parts of the house.
      * For example:
        - The mother is in the kitchen.
        - The father is in the garage.

  • In-Class Games

    • Go to

      Activity Objective:
      To practice recognizing and saying different house parts .
      Procedure:
      - Lay out the house flashcards on the floor or in different corners of the room.
      - Divide the class into two teams.
      - Pick one student from each team to be the first student.
      - The teacher will say "Go to the kitchen" or "Go to the bedroom" or "Go to the bathroom"
      - The first student from each team will run to the correct house part.
      - The first student to reach the correct house part will get a point for his/her team.
      - The teacher will continue to say "Go to the kitchen" or "Go to the bedroom" or "Go to the bathroom" until all the house parts have been used.
      - The team with the most points wins.
      Materials and Preparation: A set of house Parts flashcards

    • House Craft



      - In this activity, the students will make a house craft.
      Materials:
      - A cardboard box.
      - A pair of scissors.
      - A glue stick.
      - A marker.

      Procedure:
      - Cut the box into 4 pieces.
      - Draw a door and a window on the front of the box.
      - Glue the pieces together.
      - To Make the roof, cut a triangle out of a piece of cardboard.
      - Glue the roof on top of the box.
      - Draw a chimney on the roof.
      - To make rooms inside the house, cut a piece of cardboard into a square.
      - Glue the square on the inside of the box.
      - Draw a door and a window on the square.
      - Draw a bed, a table, and a chair inside the room.

    • Lets DrawIt



      - In this activity, the students will draw parts of the house.
      - You can draw the outside of the house and ask the students to draw the inside of the house.
      say " a bedroom" or " a kitchen" or " a bathroom" and the students will draw it.



  • Online Games.

    • Vocab Furniture

      The game is made up of 2 mini games: one matching words to images, one revealing the hidden picture.

    • Game Show

      A Multiple choice game with time pressure and life lines and a bonus round.

    • Find the Match

      - Drag and drop the words to match the pictures.

Wrap Up

- Review the vocabulary and the grammar points covered in the lesson.
- Ask the students to tell you what they learned in the lesson.
- Ask the students to tell you what they liked or disliked about the lesson.
For young learners you can simply review the vocabulary and sing a goodbye song.
