Body parts | A Complete Lesson Plan

This lesson plan should be easy to follow and understand. It is designed to be used by teachers and parents. The TEFL methodology PPP is used to teach the lesson, Which is a very popular method used by teachers. PPP stands for
Presentation, Practice and Production.

Presentation, where the teacher introduces the topic to the students.
Practice , where the teacher gives the students exercises to practice the topic.
Production , where the teacher gives the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation, for example, a conversation.
The lesson plan is divided into 3 sections, the first section is the introduction, the second section is the body of the lesson and the third section is the conclusion.

Warm Up

A warm up in this lesson is away to introduce the action verbs related to body parts.
The warm up is a game called Simon Says.
The teacher will say Simon says and then an action verb, for example, Simon says touch your nose. The students will do the action verb.
If the teacher says Touch your nose without saying Simon says, the students will not do the action verb.

The warm up time is 5 minutes max.

Remember: to explain the rules of the game to the students clearly, you can example the game by doing it yourself with the help of a volunteer student, or by using a video, also can ask the local teacher to help you.

Instead of playing a game you could use a song to warm up the students, for example, Head, shoulders, knees and toes. If you are happy and you know it. , Daddy finger , or Can You Rub Your Tummy?! , But our favorite for young learners is One Little Finger.

Practice

Practice is more about games and activities that help the students to practice the topic.
Here are some activities and games that you can use in this lesson:

Games

How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games


        Click the picture or the game title to download the file

    • Body parts mosquito game  



      Read the word and click on the correct body part, for young learners you can say the word and ask them to click on the correct body part.

    • Body Parts show

       

      A wheel of Numbers and body parts, the students will spin the wheel and say the number and the body part that matches the number.

    • The Mystery Door
      The Mystery Door Game is a fun way to get students involved in their own education by providing opportunities to make choices about what they are going to learn or do. Tell the students that behind each of the doors is a topic or theme that they can discuss during the lesson. Ask the class to decide which door to open. They should discuss their choices in English and they can make their choice by voting or nominating someone to make the decision. Once your students have opened a door, introduce the discussion topic. Next, offer your students the choice to either stick with the topic they have, or switch topics by opening a new door. The students should be aware though that if they open a new door, they might get a harder or less interesting topic.

  • In-Class Games

    • Hot Potato

      Hot Potato is a game that is played with a ball or an object that is passed from one person to another. The person who is holding the ball when the music stops is show him a picture of a body part and he has to say the name of the body part. If he says the name of the body part correctly he stays in the game, if he says the name of the body part incorrectly he is out of the game. The last person left in the game is the winner.

    • Ask and answer

      It's time for conversation practice. As the name of the game suggests, the students will ask and answer questions about the topic.
      One student will point to his body part and ask the other students "What is this?" and the other students will answer "It's a(n) _______"
      Then the other student will point to his body part and ask the other students "What is this?" and the other students will answer "It's a(n) _______"
      To make it more interesting you can ask the students to pick their partner.

  • Online Games.

    • Fast Vocabulary Game



      This is a simple game for learning basic English vocabulary. You drag the images to match the words and then click on the words to match the images. The aim of this game is provide learners with a quick and easy way to practice or review vocabulary items. The game rapidly presents images, text and audio. In this way, there is a lot of repetition and reinforcement of vocabulary. It is hoped that by being simple and quick, it can be engaging and more interesting. If students can stay engaged with the game, then they will continue to get the presentation and repetition of vocabulary items. While it is less effective if students just guess all the responses, the user does need to click on the correct word/image to progress.

    • Word search



      Word search This is a simple online word search game that students can use to practice basic English vocabulary. You have to find the words to match the images before the timer runs out.

Wrap up / Review

Ending the lesson •
Say some true or false statement about the body parts. Students clap when you make correct statements. For example:
Touch your arms and say, My arms! Students clap. Now touch your legs and say, My feet. Students remain silent.
Invite students to the board to lead the class
