Adjectives | A Complete Lesson Plan

Adjectives is one of the most important topics in ESL and EFL. It is a topic that is used in every day conversation. It is also a topic that is used in every day writing. In this lesson plan, you will learn how to teach adjectives to your students. You will also learn how to teach adjectives in a fun and engaging way. You should include a number of adjectives in every other topic you teach.
You can divide the lesson into two parts or even three. The first part is describing appearance, the second part is describing feelings, and the third part is describing objects.

Introduction

Never rush in introducing a topic, Just slow down and elicit what they know about the topic
To teach adjectives, I expect that your students already learned other topics such as body parts, family, and so on . If your students have not learned these topics, you should teach them for example body parts first. then draw a face with big eyes, small nose, big ears and so on. Then remind them of the body parts and ask them to describe the face. use gestures and facial expressions to help them describe the face.

Warm Up

AS Always, start your lesson with a warm up activity, this will help your students to get ready for the lesson. Examples of warm up activities are: Clap your hands, jump, and so on. Or a greeting song such as "hello, how are you?" or "if you are happy and you know it" and so on.

Practice

Teaching adjectives is fun and easy. You can use many activities to teach adjectives. As first time Practice you can use the following activities:

  • Practice vocabularies

    1. Pass & Say Hand a flashcard to one student and ask him to say and pass to the next etc... game

    2. Show the students the Flashcards and let them repeat after you, "clearly and Slowly"

    • "Remember to correct their pronunciation mistakes as it happens, specially the first ones"

  • Practice making full sentences

    For example, “He is strong.”, “They are young.”, etc.

  • Practice making questions

    For example, “Is he strong?” “Are they young?”


TPR

Reminder: TPR stands for Total Physical Response. It is a method of teaching that uses physical actions to help students learn.
It is a very effective method for teaching young learners.
But, Remember the students are going to imitate you. So: you have to be very clear and precise.
Be dramatic, but not over-dramatic. Be slow but not too slow

Some TPR You can use to teach adjectives:

  • Old: Act like you are pulling on a long beard as if your were an old man.

  • Young: One hand open with the level of waist .                                        

  • Bad: Point your thumbs down as a dislike

  • Good Thumbs up as a like

  • Clean: Rub your hands together as if you're cleaning them.

  • Dirty: Use facial expression as if disgusted and rub your finger tips together.

  • Tall / Short / Long:

    Tall: your hand open your arm stretched above your head.
    Short: your right hand at the level of your chest.
    Long: Use both hands to stretch your arms out horizontally.

  • High / Low Use both hands open on top of each other, for High increase the space between both hands and for Low decrease it.

  • Hard / soft
    Hard : Use both your fists forming fist bump.
    Soft: Use one finger or four finger and gently touch you skin .

How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

    • Picture Game  
      PPT. Audio flash cards on adjectives. Opposites, you can click on the paint brush to show the picture first, then let the students guess the word.

    • Hidden Picture Game  
      "A multiple choice game on colors and food. The students have to click on the word corresponding to the food. "

  • In-Class Games

    • Draw the Description

      You draw a picture of a person or an object and ask the students to describe it Example: You draw a picture of a car and ask the students to describe it.

      Also, you can play it as a charade game, divide the class into teams, and each student will draw a picture and his team will describe it.

    • 4 Corners

      Distribute the cards to the students, and ask them to stand in the four corners of the room. The teacher will call out a word, and the students will have to stand in the corner that corresponds to the word. For example, the teacher will call out the word "tall" and the students will have to stand in the corner that corresponds to the word.
      Another approach: is to ask the students to stand in the corner that corresponds to the opposite of the word.
      Also you can put objects in the corners, describe the object and ask the students to stand in the corner that corresponds to the word.

Wrap up

Wrap up the lesson by asking the students to tell you what they have learned in the lesson. For younger students, you can show them a drawing or a picture of what they have learned in the lesson. elicit the answers from the students, correct them if needed.
