Days of the Week | Lesson Plan

Teaching days of the week is a great way to introduce the concept of time to your students. This lesson plan will help you teach the days of the week in a fun and engaging way.

The Days of the week ...| by the singing Walrus

How to teach a song

Introduction

- Write the days of the week on the board.
- You can also use the flashcards below. or any calendar.
- Tell your students that you are going to teach them the days of the week.
* repeat the word "Week" several times.
- Explain what a week is.
* A week is a period of 7 days.
* Count on your fingers, and say "1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7".
- Finally, say "The days of the week are ...".
* Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, Sunday.


Flashcards of this lesson.
Click on the flashcards to preview before download.



Practice

- Say the days of the week and count on your fingers, and point to the days of the week on the board.

* Monday, 1 Tuesday, 2 Wednesday, 3 Thursday, 4 Friday, 5 Saturday, 6 Sunday, 7
- You have to say the days of the week in order, slowly and clearly.
- Repeat the days of the week several times.
- Ask your students to repeat the days of the week.
- Ask your students to point to the days of the week on the board.
- Then point to today's day of the week, and say "Today is ...".
* Today is Monday, and show one finger.
* Today is Tuesday, and show two fingers. etc...
** It works best with Asian students.
Then play " Pass the Flashcards". The students will pass the flashcards to each other and say the sentence.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity
    above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Days of the Week Activity



      - This rhythm activity is fun specially if they are familiar with food items.
      - the song included in the ppt is "Today is Monday"
      - let them listen to the song and sing it.
      - Assign every day of the week to a student.
      - Then invite the students to come in front of the class and say the day of the week.

    • What is Missing?



      - Well known , what's missing game.
      - The students will have to guess what is missing.

    • Dice Game



      - 2 students will play this game.
      - Yo will need chips or any other object for the students to mark their squares.
      - The students will have to roll the dice and say the day of the week.
      - The first student reaches the finish square wins.

  • In-Class Games

    • Raven Eyes

      How to play ?
      - Divide the students into 2 teams.
      - Stick a piece of paper or a Flashcard on the back of 2 students.
      - Write the days of the week on the paper.
      - They will to see the day on each other's back, then shout the name out loud
      - The first team to shout the name of the day wins.

    • Corners

      How to play ?
      - Spread the cards around the classroom.
      - Pick a number of students, and ask them to stand in a corner.
      - Then say "Today is Monday" and the students run to the card with Monday on it.
      - The student who gets there first wins.
      - The student who wins will be the next teacher.

    • Connect


      - You could connect the days with any other topic like food, animals, etc.
      * Example:
      On Monday we eat pizza
      On Tuesday we eat pasta
      On friday we go to the zoo
      On Saturday we go to the park

      How to play ?
      - Give the students a word. The first student says a word that they associate with the chosen word.
      - The second student says a word that they associate with the first word.
      The third student then associates a sentence with the first two words . This continues from student to student * Example:
      - Teacher says "Monday"
      - The first student says "Pizza"
      - The second student says "On Monday we eat pizza"
      * Another example:
      - Teacher says "Football"
      - The first student says "Sunday"
      - The second student says "On Sunday we play football"
      and the game continues.
      * The student who can't think of a word is out.

  • Online Games.

    • Monster Vocab



      This is a simple game for practicing days of the week in English. It gives students an chance to play, interact and listen to days language.

    • Monster Vocab



      Spin the wheel and answer the question.

    • Unjumble

      - Rearrange the days of the week in the correct order.

Wrap Up

""Quick Check""
- Ask the students to tell you the days of the week in order, Then ask them to do in reverse order.
* If you want to make it more challenging, you can ask them to do it faster.
