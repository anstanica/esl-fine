Weather | Lesson Plan

How is the weather today? What is the weather like ? , is the most common question we ask each other.It is determined by the temperature, humidity, wind speed, and precipitation. It is sunny today, it is raining, it is snowing, it is windy, it is cloudy, it is hot, it is cold and so on. Weather is something that we experience every day. It is important to know about the weather because it affects our daily lives. This lesson plan will help the ESL students to learn about the weather and how to talk about the weather.

say : What is the weather like today ?
Is it sunny ? use gestures to show the weather.
* Example : Sunny both hands up and open and your face smiling.
Cloudy Draw a cloud and your face frowning .

How to teach a song




Introduction


Draw a large temperature chart on the whiteboard and discuss the different temperatures that are usually experienced in different seasons. Ask the students what kind of weather they experienced recently and have them draw the corresponding temperature.

Questions to learn:
- How is the weather today ?
- What is the weather like today ?
TPR
- Sunny : Open one hand like a claw or a light spot .
- Cloudy : Open your hands like claws and swirl them around.
- Rainy : move your fingers from the top of your head to the bottom of your body and wiggle them like rain drops.
- Snowy : Open both hands and float them down while wiggling your fingers.
- Windy : Your open hands sway from side to side.
- Hot : Sway on hand from side to side rapidly in front of your face.
- Cold : Hug yourself and shiver.
- Stormy : Move your hands in a lightning bolt shape.
- Foggy : Move your hands Like a car's wipers.

Practice

Start by asking the students to name the different Seasons if they have already learned them. First ask the students to repeat the words after you, then ask them to say the words individually. - continue for three or four times.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity
    above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

  • In-Class Games

    • Weather Experiments



      Activity Objective:
      To practice recognizing and saying different weather types .
      Procedure: Try fun experiments with your students to demonstrate different types of weather. For example, you can make a cloud in a jar to show them how clouds are formed and how rain is created. You can also make a tornado in a bottle to show them how tornadoes are formed.

      How to make a cloud in a jar:
      - Fill a jar with water.
      - Add a few drops of dish soap.
      - Add a few drops of food coloring.
      - Put the lid on the jar and shake it.
      - The water will turn cloudy.
      - The cloud will disappear when you open the lid.

      How to make a tornado in a bottle:
      Fill your container about ¾ full with clean water.
      Add a squirt of dish soap, as well as your glitter or food coloring, if desired
      Seal the container tightly.
      Move the bottle rapidly in a circular motion. After a few seconds, you should see the water begin swirling. Stop, and watch your tornado!

      Materials and Preparation:
      - A jar or a bottle.
      - Water.
      - Dish soap.
      - Food coloring. or Glitter

    • Weather Bingo



      Create bingo cards with weather-related words such as 'cloud', 'rain', 'snow', 'tornado', and 'sun'.( included in the PPT file).
      it's a good idea to have a few extra cards in case some students don't have a partner.

      Procedure:
      - Divide the class into pairs.
      - Give each pair a bingo card.
      - Tell the students to take turns calling out the weather-related words on their cards.
      - The first student to get five words in a row wins.

    • Memory Game



      - Put up pictures of different kinds of weather and have the kids take turns trying to remember which pictures are associated with each kind of weather.
      - For example, you can put up a picture of a sunny day and a picture of a rainy day.
      - The kids will have to remember which picture is associated with which kind of weather.
      For older students, you can also have them match the pictures with the correct weather words.

    • Roll The Dice



      - Create a dice with pictures of different kinds of weather on each side.
      - Have the kids roll the dice and then say the weather that is associated with the picture that they rolled.
      - For example, if they roll a picture of a sunny day, they should say "It's sunny today."
      * The dice cube is included in the worksheet file.



  • Online Games.

    • The weather

      This is a simple game for learning English. The target language is weather vocabulary, countries and weather related phrases; the aim of the game is to have student exposed to weather language and to practice phrases related to weather For example, the vocabulary items "Sunny", "Rain", "Hot" and "Cold", and weather phrases such as "in England it Will be cold" or "in France it Will be sunny". There are 4 sets of content, with each using different kinds of weather vocabulary and countries from different parts of the world. The first group uses the basic weather items nd some countries from Europe. The second set is weather describing how hot it is and uses countries from Asia_ The third set is more obscure weather vocabulary, such as "drizzle" and It uses directions on a map of North America. Finally, the last set uses a map of the whole world and involves disaster weather vocab such as "Flood" and "Forest fire".

Wrap Up

create a weather forecast! Have the kids brainstorm different types of weather they know, like sunny, rainy, windy, snowy, etc. Then, have each student pick one type of weather and draw it or talk about it in front of the class.
