Daily Routine | Lesson Plan

Daily routine is a set of activities that people do on a regular basis at the same time every day, such as eating and sleeping, going to work or school. It is important to teach your students about daily routines. You can use it as a daily part of your upcoming lessons. and in circle time for daily chit chat with your students.

Warm Up

- Ask your students to stand up and start exercising.
- Start with simple exercises like jumping, running, and stretching.
- Then add daily routine instructions, and act it out,
Example:
wash your hands / face , brush your teeth, comb your hair, put on your clothes, eat breakfast, go to school, go to bed, etc.
- It is important to ask your students to repeat after you, not just acting out
- continue for 5 minutes.

Introduction

This lesson we will use a song to introduce daily routine.
- Ask your students to sing the song with you.
- After listening to the song, ask your students to tell you what they know about daily routine.

Practice

To practice the daily routine, we can use the flashcards . Next show them Flashcards one by one and act it out. Ask the students to do the same.
Then play " Pass the Flashcards". The students will pass the flashcards to each other and say the sentence.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity
    above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

  • In-Class Games

    • Pogo Drill

      How to play ?
      - Two students sit facing each other.
      - The first student says a sentence.
      - The second student says the sentence again and adds a new sentence.
      - The first student says the sentence again and adds a new sentence.
      Example:
      - Student A: I wake up at 6:00 am.
      - Student B: I wake up at 6:00 am. I wash my hands.
      - Student A: I wake up at 6:00 am. I wash my hands. I brush my teeth.
      - Student B: I wake up at 6:00 am. I wash my hands. I brush my teeth. I eat breakfast.
      and so on.
      It could be challenging at first but it will get easier with practice. To make it easier, allow them to use the flashcards.

    • Mix and Match

      How to play ?
      Mix and Match involves having a number of words written on cards, with picture cards that correspond.
      - In other words, if the word on the card is "brush your teeth", you should have another card with a picture of the word. It is usually best to have no more than 20 matching pairs.
      - The words and pictures are randomly placed, face side down, on a board or on the ground.
      - Invite a learner to turn over only two cards, instructing the leaner that if he or she finds a matching pair, he or she can continue to look for more pails. Learners must then remove a single picture or word and try to match it

    • Flyswatter


      - Divide students into two teams. Each team is given a single marker and takes turns choosing a leader, who is handed a flyswatter.
      - The leader and group must listen carefully to a definition of a word. A number of words are written randomly across the board.
      - Tell students that the leader must choose the correct word by “swatting” the correct word faster than his or her opponent.
      - The first person to swat the word receives a point for his/her team.

  • Online Games.

    • Monster Vocab



      Monster Vocab is a fun way to practice vocabulary.
      - 30 questions divided in to 2 parts.
      - First part, click on the correct picture that matches the word.
      - Second part, drag the picture into the correct box that matches the word.

    • Concentration

      - The aim is match words and images by revealing corresponding pairs.
      - The items are hidden behind squares and are revealed when clicked.
      - To match all the pairs, you have to concentrate and remember where the items are.

    • Wack a mole



      - Wack a mole is a game where you have to hit the mole with a hammer.
      - The mole will pop up from the holes and you have to hit the correct one with the hammer.
