Verbs| Lesson Plan

There are three types of verbs in English language. They are action verbs, linking verbs and helping verbs.

Action verbs are verbs that show an action. For example,run, jump, swim, eat, drink, sleep etc ...
Linking verbs are verbs that describe the subject rather than the action by connecting it to a predicate adjective or predicate noun. For example, is, am, are, was, were, be, been, seem, appear, feel, look, sound, taste, smell etc ...
Helping Verbs are verbs that help the main verb to express an action. For example, can, could, may, might, must, shall, should, will, would, ought to, etc ...
Remember we use TEFL methodology to teach Verbs to students

Presentation, Practice and Production.

Presentation : where the teacher introduces the topic to the students.
Practice : where the teacher gives the students exercises to practice the topic.
Production : where the teacher gives the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation.

Introduction

First , it would be helpful to review the basics of verbs. Explain that verbs are words that describe an action, state, or occurrence. For example, "run," "be," and "help" are all verbs. You could also provide a few examples of verbs in a sentence.
Next, it would be beneficial to focus on the different types of verbs. Explain the three main categories: action verbs, linking verbs, and helping verbs. Give examples of each and explain how they differ.

Provide simple examples of verbs in a sentence. This will help the students understand how to use verbs in a sentence. for example:

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what color is this?
    Student B: It's red.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

Wrap up / Review

Congratulations on teaching your ESL verbs lesson! Wrapping up the lesson is an important step to ensure that the material is reinforced and that the students understand the material they just learned. Here are some ideas for how to do this:

  1. Have the students create a story using the new verbs they just learned. This way they can practice putting the words in context and will be more likely to remember them.
  2. Have a quiz at the end of the lesson to check for understanding. This could be as simple as having the students write a sentence using one of the verbs from the lesson or having them pick out the verb from a list of other words.
  3. Finish the lesson with a review game. This could be something like charades, where the students act out the verb and the other students try to guess it, or it could be a more structured game like Jeopardy.
