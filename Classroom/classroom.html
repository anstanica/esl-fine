Classroom| Lesson Plan

Classroom objects are the things that are used in a classroom. They are used by the teacher and the students. They are used to teach and learn. They are used to make the classroom a better place to learn.
Remember we use TEFL methodology to teach Classroom Objects to students

Presentation, Practice and Production.

Presentation : where the teacher introduces the topic to the students.
Practice : where the teacher gives the students exercises to practice the topic.
Production : where the teacher gives the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation.

Warm Up

It's important to teach Classroom Objects TO students .To give them instructions like :
turn on the light, turn off the light, open your books, open the door, close the door, bring your pencil, put your pencil on the table, put your pencil in the pencil case. And so on.

The warm-up could a simple exercise first, Then watching a video, The video will help the students.

Introduction

We start by showing them a picture of their own school, and point to the picture and say:
"This is our school" repeat the word school. Then Show them a picture of a classroom and say "This is our classroom" repeat the word classroom.

Once the students see pictures of their school and classroom, they are know got the idea of the topic.
And, finally show them a picture of the classroom objects or point to the objects and say:
"These are the classroom objects", today we are going to learn the names of the classroom objects: for example:
chair, table, pencil, book, pen, eraser, ruler, blackboard, chalk, clock, door, window, light, etc.

of different Classroom and asking them to name the Classroom.

Practice

First The teacher, first needs to elicit the names of the classroom objects from the students. You can use the flashcards from above. . Or You can just point to the objects and ask the students, to check if they know the English words.

In this step, the teacher should be patient and wait for the students to answer.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what is this?
    Student B: It's a pencil.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

You can apply the practice in any order you want.
But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

Action verbs for this lesson:


Point,carry, draw, type, write, read, sharpen, erase, sit, open, close and stick

Grammar with this lesson:


Articles and plurals:
It's a pencil. It's an eraser.
It's a book. They are books.

Below are some activities for practicing this lesson:

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Cross The River



      Choose the correct answer to cross the river, otherwise you will fall into the water.

    • Spin the Wheel

       

      Spin the wheel and choose the correct answer.

    • Mouse Trap    

       

      The students will choose the correct answer to avoid the cat trap.
      Use this game for practicing articles and plurals.

  • In-Class Games

    • Chinese whispers


      Make a circle and whisper a word or sentence to one student.
      The word must go ‘round the circle and the student on your other side must whisper to you what he heard.
      Write the sentence you said and the sentence this student said. If they’re the same, stars for everyone.

    • Dirty Monkey

      A - Make two students sit on the floor in front of each other leaving enough space between them to place three flashcards.
      B - Place the three flashcards between them and give them time to think about the words they have in front of them.
      C - Once they look ready, loudly say the name on one flashcard. The first one to touch it wins and stays there and the loser goes to sit down.
      D - Give one star each; as the winner stays there he can get as many stars as he can as long as he keeps on winning.

    • Basketball

      A box or basket in the center of the classroom
      A circle around it
      They throw a ball from outside of the circle after reproducing vocabulary
      If it enters the box, star!

  • Online Games.

    • Vocab game



      The aim is practice seeing and hearing the vocabulary. And then to associate these with the pictures.
      The game is made up of 2 mini games: one matching words to images, one revealing the hidden picture.

    • Monster Vocab



      - TO play you click on the correct the picture that matches the word.
      - You click at the top right of the screen to turn off the sound.

    • Big Describer



      To play, you read the brief description and then click on the image that it best describes.
