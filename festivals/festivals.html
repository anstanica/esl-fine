"Reminder" at the beginning of every new topic, you have to elicit the vocabularies they know. You can do this by asking them or writing down the words,then asking them to point to the words they know. or showing the flashcards and asking them. Usually, you can elicit 5-10 words.

Introduction

You act like you are driving a car, bus, train, plane, boat, ship, bicycle, motorcycle, truck, taxi, helicopter, submarine, rocket, spaceship, and so on. Don't show them the pictures, just act like you are driving a car or a bus using the horn and the steering wheel. Then, ask them to imitate you. Then you can show them the flashcards, and elicit the vocabularies.

Warm Up

Always a good warm up activity to get the students engaged and ready to learn is singing a song related to the topic.
Example "Transportation song - car bus taxi - The Wheels on the Bus ".

Practice

The main focus of this lesson is asking and answering this question ‘How do you go to school?‘. you start by telling them how do you go to school, then you ask them how do they go to school. Then you can ask them to write the answer in the worksheet. Say the names out loud and ask students to repeat after you. repeat each word 3 times. A perfect approach is playing Pass & Say Hand a flashcard to one student and ask him to say and pass to the next etc... game

"Remember to correct their pronunciation, specially the first ones"

TPR

  • on foot: walk on the spot.

  • by bus: pretend to drive a bus and beep the horn.

  • by bicycle: pretend to be cycling and ring a bell.

  • by car: pretend to drive a car and beep the horn.

  • by train: do the train gesture – moving your hands around like a wheel and “chu-chu”ing.

  • by boat: do wave motions with your hands.

  • by airplane: pretend to be an airplane by holding your arms right out.

  • by rocket: put your arms above your head, finger tips touching to make a rocket shape. Keep this shape for the countdown and then jump for the blast off.

  • In-Class Games

    • Spin the bottle

      The same idea as the "Board Game" but this time you can play it with flashcards.

      • Students will sit in a circle.

      • Lay the flashcards inside the circle in a circle shape and use a water bottle or any other object and ask the students to take turns in spinning the bottle, and the bottle cap will be the pointer.

      • The student will take a flashcard that the bottle cap pointed at then say the word and/or the sentence, the next student will spin the bottle and so on. "

      Another Approach:

      - If you have enough flashcards you can hand every student a flashcard.
      (make sure every student know the word, if he doesn't you can ask them to repeat 2 or three times)
      - A student spins the bottle and asks a question? Example "How do you come/go to school", The student who the pointer points to will answer.
      You can deduct points or add points accordingly.

    • Memory Game

      Put the flashcards face down on a table or on the floor, after showing them to the students or you can cover them with blank papers, then ask the students "Where is a car ?" OR "Which one is a bus ?"

    • Toys

      Get a box fill it with food toys ,ask the students put their hands inside the box and guess the name of the food. , and ask the student to show it to the class, if the student is correct, he will keep the food toy, and if he is incorrect, he will put it back

Wrap up

Wrap up the lesson by asking the students to tell you what they have learned in the lesson. For younger students, you can ask them to draw a picture of the food they have learned in the lesson. elicit the answers from the students, correct them if needed.
