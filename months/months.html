Months | Lesson Plan

Teaching months could be a bit tricky for some teachers, but with the right strategies in place, they can be an enjoyable and rewarding experience. We have created a lesson plan for you to use in your classroom to teach months. This lesson plan is designed to be used with students in kindergarten and first grade.

Introduction

- Before you start teaching the months, you should review the seasons and the days of the week . Then Show your students a calendar and talk about the different months and what season they belong to. Then introduce the main concept of the months of the year. Explain that there are 12 months in a year and that each month has a special name.

Questions to learn:
- What is the name of the month?
- What is the name of the season?
- what month is it now?
- what month is your birthday?
- what is your favorite month?
- what is your least favorite month?
- what is the name of the month that comes before?
- what is the name of the month that comes after?

Practice

Ask the children to tell you which month is their birthday use a calendar to help them. This will help them learn the months in relation to themselves, which can help with the overall understanding of the concept.
Next : teach the months of the year in order and ask the children to repeat after you. Or you can play "touch and say game" where you hold up a card with a month on it and the children touch it and say the name of the month.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity
    above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

  • In-Class Games

    These games can be fun and engaging ways to help students learn more about the different months.

    • Banana
      Start by asking the students to stand up and form a circle. Then, assign each student a month. The first student will be "January" and the last student will be "December".
      Assign a student to be the "banana". The banana will say "banana" and not the name of the month.
      if the student says the name of the month, they are out.
      The last student standing is the winner.
      The months should be in order, so the students can remember the months.
      To make it more challenging, you can have the students say the months faster.

    • Matching Game
      - Create a game board with pictures of different events that happen in each month (snow in January, flowers in April, xmas in December etc.) and have the kids match the pictures to the months.

    • Months in order
      Lay out a bunch of flashcards with the months on them. Have the kids put them in order.
      You can also have them write the months in order on a piece of paper.

    • Calendar Race
      Draw a simple calendar on the board and split the kids into two teams. Have the teams race to see who can write the names of each of the months in the correct order the fastest.
      You can use flashcards and play race and touch instead of writing.
