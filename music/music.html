Music | Lesson Plan

Teaching music instruments to ESL students can be both a rewarding and challenging experience. It requires patience, creativity, and an understanding of how to best communicate with your students.
Here are some tips to help you get started: Start with the basics:
Before introducing any instruments, it's important to provide a basic background and overview of music. This can include information on music theory, the different types of instruments, and the way they produce sound.
Use visuals:
Visuals can be a great way to help ESL students understand the concepts and techniques associated with playing music instruments.

Objectives

  • To learn about the different types of music instruments.

Warm Up

A great warmup activity for teaching musical instruments is to have them create their own rhythms.
Sounds not easy right?
It is not as hard as you think.
You can do this by having them tap out a rhythm with their hands, clapping, and/or using other household items like spoons or pencils.
* For example :
Have them tap out a rhythm on their knees. Then have them clap their hands. then have them clap their hands and tap their knees at the same time.
Have them repeat the rhythm a few times and then have them create their own.
It is gonna be chaos and fun at the same time.
You can also have them use a metronome to help them stay in time. Once they have created a rhythm, have them name it and use it to play an instrument.
You can have them play the same rhythm on different instruments to show how different instruments create different sounds. This is a great way to introduce musical instruments to ESL kids and get them excited about playing music.

Introduction

Introducing musical instruments to children can be a fun and educational experience! One great way to get them started is by showing them videos or images of different instruments, and talking about the sounds they make and how they are used. Then, you can have them try something simple like shaking a tambourine or playing some notes on a keyboard. As they get more comfortable, you can introduce them to more complex instruments like guitars or drums.

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Play Music



      A musical game the students can listen and play the music directly in the PPT
      two songs "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star" and "Ba ba black sheep"

    • What are they playing?



      - Listen to the music and guess what instrument is playing.

  • In-Class Games

    These games can be fun and engaging ways to help students learn more about the different instruments.

    • "Name That Instrument"

      - Divide the class into two teams and have one student from each team come up to the front of the room.
      - Give each student a different instrument /or they can mime playing and have the other team guess what instrument it is.
      You can also have the student playing the instrument explain a bit about it and how it works.
      Materials and Preparation: A set of music instruments or flashcards.

    • Musical Chairs



      Musical Chairs" where each chair has an instrument Flashcard on it. As the music plays, the students have to race around the chairs and sit in them. When the music stops,
      the student sitting in the chair has to explain the instrument they are sitting on.

    • Listen and identify



      - You can do a simple listening activity where the students listen to recordings of different instruments and identify which one they are hearing.



  • Online Games.

    • Monster vocab

    • Yes Or No

    • Match

Wrap Up

-You could play a game of musical instruments charades. Divide the students into two teams, and have each team take turns guessing musical instrument names based on their teammates' acting. Give each team a set amount of time to come up with a guess. At the end of the game, the team with the most correct guesses wins!
