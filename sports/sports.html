This lesson plan is designed to introduce children to the basics of sports. It is intended to help them understand sports and the skills needed to play them.

Warm Up

A great warmup for teaching a sports lesson to preschoolers is to start out with some fun stretching exercises. Have the kids spread out and do some easy stretches like arm circles, leg swings, and toe touches. Then, you can lead them in some fun aerobic exercises like jumping jacks, running in place, and hopping. After that, you can bring them together and have them practice simple gross motor skills like hopping, skipping, galloping, and running. Finally, you can end with a game like "Simon Says" or "Red Light, Green Light" to get them laughing and having fun.

Introduction

Start by introducing the students to the concept of sports. Explain that sports involve physical activity, competition, and rules. Show pictures of various sports and ask the students to name each one. Ask them about the rules for each sport and the equipment needed to play it.

Questions:
- what is your favorite sport?
- what is the name of this sport?
- is this sport a team sport or an individual sport?
- is it basketball or volleyball?

Practice

Have the students practice saying the names of the sports out loud. Make sure to provide positive feedback for their efforts.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity
    above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

  • In-Class Games

    • “Basketball Hot Potato”

      This game is perfect for a group of kids. Have them start by standing in a circle and passing a basketball around. When the music stops, whoever is holding the ball ask him a question (What sport is this ?). Keep playing until there is only one person left.

    • Marble Race

      Have the preschoolers make a track with the flashcards, masking tape, and paper cups. Then, have them take turns rolling marbles down the track, if the marble falls off the track, ask them a question about the topic, if they answer correctly, they can roll again.

    • Scrabble, Boggle Games

      Scrabble and Boggle are two classic word games that are fun for all ages.
      - Scrabble is a board game where players take turns placing letter tiles on a board to spell out words. Points are awarded for words depending on the letter values, and bonus points can also be earned.
      - Boggle is a word game where players race against each other to find as many words as possible in a 4x4 grid of letters. The player with the most points at the end of the game is the winner.
      Both Scrabble and Boggle are great for improving spelling and vocabulary skills, as well as providing hours of entertainment.

Wrap Up

Ask the students to explain what they learned about sports in this lesson. Initiate a discussion about the importance of physical activity and the role sports can play in staying healthy. Remind the students of the importance of following the rules and playing fair when participating in sports.
