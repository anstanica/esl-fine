Numbers

Numbers| A Complete Lesson Plan

Numbers are the most important part of our life. We use numbers in our daily life. We use numbers to count things, to measure things, to tell time, to tell our age, to tell our weight, to tell our height, to tell our phone number, That's why we should to teach numbers to students the very first topics in the school.
Remember we use TEFL methodology to teach numbers to students (PPP). Presentation, Practice and Production.

Presentation, where the teacher introduces the topic to the students.
Practice , where the teacher gives the students exercises to practice the topic.
Production , where the teacher gives the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation, for example, a conversation.

Warm Up

If your students are familiar with numbers, you can start with a warm up activity. You can ask them to count the number of students in the class, or the number of chairs in the class, or the number of windows in the class, and so on. Or if they are not familiar with numbers, but they have learnt some exercise actions example: Jump, clap, run, and so on. You can ask them to do the exercise action the number of times you say. For example, if you say 5, they should do the exercise action 5 times. If you say 10, they should do the exercise action 10 times. And so on.

The warm up time is 5 minutes max.

Remember: to explain the rules of the game to the students clearly, you can example the game by doing it yourself with the help of a volunteer student, or by using a video, also can ask the local teacher to help you.

Introduction

To introduce Numbers for the first time to the students, point to your fingers and start counting from 1 to 10. Then ask the students to do the same.
Next show them Flashcards and ask them to repeat after you several times.

Practice

Practice is more about games and activities that help the students to practice the topic.
Here are some activities and games that you can use in this lesson:

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Numbers & colors game



      1-20 and basic colors. Pupils listen to the colors and then click on the right coloured pencil.

    • How many can you see?

       

      practise numbers 1-10 and toys.

  • In-Class Games

    • Banana

      Banana is one the most loved game in the ESL classroom. It is a great way to practice numbers and counting. Students stand or sit in a circle, the teacher gives numbers to the students in order, or He can let them count in order. The teacher choose a number to be the banana, let's say 5., the student who is number 5 should stand up and say "banana" instead of 5 and sit down, and the count continues. The teacher changes the number of the banana every 2 or 3 rounds. If the student who is the banana says the number instead of banana, he/she is out of the game. The last student standing is the winner.

    • Missing Number

      This game is a great way to practice writing the numbers and counting.
      The teacher writes numbers on the board or a screen in a order first and then erases some of them. The teacher asks the students to write the missing numbers. The students can work in pairs or individually.

  • Online Games.

    • Fast Vocabulary Game



      This is a simple game for learning basic English vocabulary. The aim of this game is provide learners with a quick and easy way to practice or review vocabulary items. The game rapidly presents images, text and audio. In this way, there is a lot of repetition and reinforcement of vocabulary. It is hoped that by being simple and quick, it can be engaging and more interesting. If students can stay engaged with the game, then they will continue to get the presentation and repetition of vocabulary items. While it is less effective if students just guess all the responses, the user does need to click on the correct word/image to progress.

    • Monster Numbers



      This is a mini game for learning English. Read the text or listen to the audio and then click on the matching image. You can choose from 24 different sets of content or add r own images from you desktop. The countdown timer is meant to add an element of challenge to make it more exciting.

Wrap up / Review

Ending the lesson •
Say some true or false statement about the numbers. You can use fingers or flashcards to help the students. Students clap when you make correct statements. For example:
Show 3 fingers and say, 3! Students clap. Now 5 fingers and say, 4. Students remain silent.
Invite students to the board to lead the class
