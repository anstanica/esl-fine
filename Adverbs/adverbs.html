Adverbs

Adverbs| Lesson Plan

adverbs are words that describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They tell us how, when, where, or to what extent something happens.

Examples:
How
He quickly ran to the store.
When
I always go to the store on Saturdays.
Where
The store is near my house.
To what extent
I really like the store.
How to teach adverbs
1. Introduce the topic to the students.
2. Give the students exercises to practice the topic.
3. Give the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation.

Remember we use TEFL methodology to teach adverbs to students

Presentation, Practice and Production.

Presentation : where the teacher introduces the topic to the students.
Practice : where the teacher gives the students exercises to practice the topic.
Production : where the teacher gives the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation.

Introduction

We suppose that you already taught adjectives, if not you can check our adjectives lesson plan. You will then guide your students to convert adjectives into adverbs using certain rules.

    Adverbs come in regular and irregular forms:

  • Regular adverbs: adjective + ly quickly, slowly, carefully, quietly, loudly, etc.

  • Irregular adverbs: fast -> fast, high → high, low → low, good → well

    • Adverbs Forms
  • Adverbs of manner: quickly, slowly, carefully, quietly, loudly, etc.
  • Adverbs of place: here, there, nearby, above, under, etc.
  • Adverbs of time: now, then, tomorrow, yesterday, etc.
  • Adverbs of frequency: always, never, sometimes, often, etc.
  • Adverbs of degree: very, too, enough, etc.

Now comes the fun part – introducing the adverbs.

Ask the students to line up and tell them that they are going to play a game.
"Cho - cho" is a game where the students are going to be a train.
The teacher is the driver and the students are the passengers.

As the train is moving around the classroom, shout out the following commands and have everyone follow what you do:

  • "Let’s go quickly!" – speed up - also speed up chanting "1, 2, 1, 2, …".
  • "Let’s go slowly!" – slow right down to a very slow pace and also slow down chanting "1 … 2 … 1 … 2 …".
  • "Let’s go high!" – put your hands high up in the air as the train moves around the class.

Keep moving around the class and changing the instructions. It is great fun and an exciting way to begin the lesson.

Practice

To practice the adverbs, we can use the flashcards . Next show them Flashcards and ask them to repeat after you several times.
Then use play pass the flashcards. The students will pass the flashcards to each other and say the color.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what color is this?
    Student B: It's red.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Adjective or Adverb game



      Is it an adjective or adverb.

    • Adverbs of Manner

       

      Choose the correct answer.

  • In-Class Games

    • High and Low

      - You need a set of verbs and adverbs flashcards.
      - Hold the verb flashcard up, and ask the students to say the verb out loud and do the action. Example, "Run".
      - Then hold the adverb flashcard down and ask the students to say the adverb quietly. Example, "Quickly".
      - Keep doing this until you have gone through all the flashcards, but every time change the pace from slow to fast.

    • The Flyswatter Adverb Game

      A - Write down a bunch of adverbs on the whiteboard in random fashion.
      B -Then, one student from each team comes up to the front and grabs a flyswatter. Say another word and students have to choose an adverb that could modify it well. For example, on the whiteboard, there might be the word slowly.
      Then, you could say walk and students could slap the word slowly but they couldn’t slap accidentally. Replace the adverb with another one and the game continues with the next students.

    • Charades with Verbs and Adverbs

      This is like a typical charade game but it uses verbs and adverbs.

      - The teacher says, "I am going to act out a verb and an adverb. You have to guess what they are."
      - The teacher acts out the verb and adverb. For example, the teacher could act out the verb "run" and the adverb "quickly".
      - The students have to guess what the verb and adverb are.
      - The teacher then acts out another verb and adverb and so on.
      For example:
      - run quickly
      - walk slowly
      - jump high
      - swim fast
      - eat slowly
      - talk loudly
      - write slowly
      - read quickly
      - sleep deeply
      - play quietly
      - sing loudly
      - dance slowly
      - drive quickly
      - walk slowly

Wrap up / Review

You can wrap up the lesson by playing a game and by doing a review.
For example, you can play a game of : “You Say I act out.

Ask the students to say a verb and adverb and then act it out.
For example:
- They say: “run quickly” - Then You act it out.
- Then they say: “walk slowly” and so on.
