Countries | Lesson Plan

Teaching Countries to young learners can be a challenge. This lesson plan will help you teach Countries to your students in a fun and engaging way.
Why teaching Countries is important?
Countries are the places where we live, work, and play. Where we have our families and friends. Where we have our homes and schools.
Students need to know what other countries are like, and how they are different from their own, in order to understand the world around them.

Introduction

First, Display the world map and check if the learners know what it is and what countries are. You could ask ‘What is this?’ to elicit, ‘It’s a map of the world’. Do the same for relevant countries
Second, Point to your own country on the map and say the name of it. Example: ‘This is the USA.’ then point to your self and say: I am from the USA. or your name (Peter) is from the USA.
Then, point to their country and say the name of it. Example: ‘This is China’ and then point to the student and say: ‘You are from China.’

Practice

To practice the Countries, we can use the flashcards . Next show them Flashcards one by one and act it out. Ask the students to do the same.
Then use play pass the flashcards. The students will pass the flashcards to each other and say the word.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity
    above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

  • In-Class Games

    • Role Play

      How to play ?
      - Bring a globe of the world map.
      - Then Pick 2 students to spin the globe.
      - One student spins the globe and points to a country, then asks the       other student "Where are you from?"
      - The other will answer "I am from (country name)".
      - They will take turns asking and answering.
      * You can divide the class into groups and play the game in groups.

    • Hot Potato Countries around the World Game

      - Students pass around an object and the person holding it when the music stops or the timer goes off has to Answer a question about the country.

    • Join the Dots


      - Draw a five-by-five dotted grid on the board.
      - Divide the class into teams, and give each team a different a country name.
      - Take it in turns to ask each team a quiz question.
      - If a team answers correctly, they connect two of the dots with a line     either horizontally or vertically.
      - The aim of the game is to join the dots together to make squares. When   a team forms a square as a result of drawing a fourth line, they write   their team's country in the square and have another turn.
      - The game is over when all 16 squares have been completed. The team with the most squares wins the game.

  • Online Games.

    • Fast Phrases



      - This is a game for practicing English sentences.
      - Look at the images and make sentences to describe what you see.
      - To make the sentences, you have to choose from the options shown. If you click on the correct one, then that part of the sentence is added.
      - Points are awarded for correct hits and this multiplies if you continue to get the answers right.
      * The idea behind this game is that the meaning of the images becomes associated with the sentence you construct. When making the phrase, the vocabulary is reinforced and the grammatical structure is practiced.

    • Countries Maze

      - This is a simple Maze game for practicing countries.
      - To play, you click on the start button and then use the arrow keys to move the character around the maze.
      - The aim of the game is to get to the correct country.

    • Falling Countries



      You are presented with a jumbled sentence and have to re-arrange the words to create an correct sentence. The idea is that by re-ordering the words you can practice grammar points and also acquire vocabulary. ages appear at the top of the screen to provide meaning/context to the sentence. If you can correctly arrange the clouds within a few moves, then you get a score bonus.

    • Airplane Travel



      - Fly the airplane to the correct country.
      - To play, you click on the start button and then use the arrow keys to move the airplane around the screen.
