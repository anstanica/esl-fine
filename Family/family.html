Family | Lesson Plan

Family is a group of people who are related to each other by blood, marriage, or adoption. It is the basic unit of society.
It is the first school of a child. It is the first place where a child learns good and bad things. It is the first place where a child learns to speak, walk, eat, and do other things.

How to teach a song

Introduction

- First we need to elicit what the students know about family, because most likely they will know something about it.
- Next show the students a picture of your family, point to each member and say who they are.
- Then show them the family tree picture, "Included in the worksheets section", and say "This is family tree, it shows family members and how we are related to each other".

* Then let's practice.

TPR

    - "Father" Put your finger under your nose like a mustache.
    - "Mother" :Both hands on your hair like a ponytail.
    - "Brother" : Show right hand's 'middle and index fingers' and stick them   together.
    - "Sister" : Show left hand's 'middle and index fingers' and stick them     together.
    - "Grandfather" : ACT like you are holding a walking stick, and walk       slowly.
    - "Grandmother" : Put both hands behind your back and walk slowly.

Practice

- Family fingers puppets, you can buy them online or make them yourself.
- Wear the finger puppets and say the words and sentences, ask the students to repeat.
- Then pass the finger puppets to the students and ask them to say the words and sentences.
- Then play " Pass the Flashcards". The students will pass the flashcards to each other and say the sentence.
- Next is Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs to practice " I have ".
example:
Student A : "I have a brother"
Student B : "I have a sister"


  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity
    above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what do you?
    Student B: I am a doctor.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Family Activity



      - Multiple choice game on family members reviewing the family members and their relationships.

    • Family At Home



      - Multiple choice game on family members and home. - where are they?

  • In-Class Games

    You can play Charades, Pictionary, or "Literally" any other game that you like, and it will be fit.
    But Family members topic is more about activities and not games.
    Here are some ideas:
    - Ask the students to write a short story about their family.
    - Ask the students to draw a picture of their family.
    - Ask the students to bring a family photo and tell the class about it.
    - Draw a family tree on the board and ask the students to write the names of their family members.

  • Online Games.

    • Labelled Diagram



      Drag and Drop the pins to the correct place on the image.

    • Matching pairs

      - Tap a pair of tiles to reveal if they match.

    • Unscramble

      - Drag and drop the keywords to the correct description.

Wrap Up

- To wrap up the lesson, ask the students to write a short story about their family.
- Sing "Family finger song" for younger learners.
