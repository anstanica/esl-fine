Places| Lesson Plan

What is a place?
A place is a location.

- Places like :
a school, a hospital, a park, a beach,a zoo, a museum, a library, a restaurant, a hotel, a bank, a police station, a fire station, a pharmacy and a gas station.

- Teaching places to students is very important because it helps them to know where they can go to get different services.

- where they can go to have fun, to learn and to get help in case of an emergency. - where are their parents work.

Remember we use TEFL methodology to teach places to students

Presentation, Practice and Production.

Presentation : where the teacher introduces the topic to the students.
Practice : where the teacher gives the students exercises to practice the topic.
Production : where the teacher gives the students a chance to use the topic in a real life situation.

Introduction

- If you have a poster of the places, you can show it to the students. If you don't have a poster, you can draw a picture of the places on the board.
- Point to the school in the poster and ask the students , if they know where they are.
- Then, explain to them that we are going to learn about places. Ask them if they know any places.
If they know any, write them on the board.
If they don't know any, you can tell them some places.
For example, you can tell them that you go to school, you go to the park, you go to the hospital.

Another idea is to build a story about a person who goes to different places.
For example :
A boy / a girl went to the post office to send a letter to his friend, but he didn't have any money.
so, he went to the bank to get some money. But he didn't have an account.
so he went back home to ask his mother for some money. But his mother wasn't at home, she was at the market.
So he called his father to ask him for some money,but his father was at the hospital, he is a doctor
So he went to the park to wait for his mother, When his mother came back, she gave him some money. Then he went to the post office to send the letter.

And include visual aids of the places in the story.

Practice

To practice the places, we can use the flashcards . Next show them Flashcards and ask them to repeat after you several times.
Then use play pass the flashcards. The students will pass the flashcards to each other and say the color.

  • Isolation practice : isolation practice as mentioned in the activity above (Asking and practicing one by one).

  • Pair practice : where the students will work in pairs. example:
    Student A: what color is this?
    Student B: It's red.

  • Group practice : as the name suggests is where the students will work in groups. group games , group activities, and group points.

    • You can apply the practice in any order you want.
    But we recommend that you start with the isolation practice, then the pair practice, and finally the group practice.

Games


How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • Places Around



      Audio flash cards on places around town. You can use it both to show the vocabulary on this topic and to check it

    • Maze

       

      Who goes where?
      - The students will follow the lines to find out where each person goes.

    • Hidden Picture Game

       

      Click on the square to reveal parts of the picture

  • In-Class Games

    • Coin Race A drilling Game

      - This activity game is ideal for practicing question and answer         structures.
      The game works best with small class sizes.
      - Write numbered question and answer structures on the board.
      - There should be one question and answer pattern for each student in the   class.
      - Sit the students in a circle. Have the students repeat the first         question on the board three times.
      - When the students have repeated the question a third time, start to       spin a coin on the floor.
      - As the coin spins, the students answer the question in turn going         around the circle.
      - When the coin stops spinning, the student whose turn it is to speak,     answers the question and is out of the game.
      - Erase the first question and answer structure off the board and then     repeat the game with the second question and answer structure.
      - When the coin stops, the student whose turn it is to speak answers the   first and second question and then leaves the game.
      - This process is repeated until there is one student left. That student   wins the game.

    • Make a Sentence

      - Each team stands in a line. Hold up a flashcard and make a sentence       based on the picture.
      - For example, "school" and then say "I go to school every day."
         Or "I go to school by bus."
        "library" and then say " I read books at the library."
      - The teams then repeat the sentence three times and sit down. The team     that sits down first scores a point.
      - Then, repeat the game, but this time have the students do an action       before they sit down,
         - e.g. clap and then sit, jump and then sit, etc. Repeat the game with   a different action or actions each time.
      - As the game progresses, instead of every member of the team speaking at the same time, each student repeats the sentence three times, do an action and then sit down.
      * The idea is to make the tasks more challenging as the game progresses. The team with the most points at the end of the game wins.

    • Flashcards at Dawn


      - One student from each team comes to the front of the class.
      - Have the two students stand back to back.
      - Give each student a flashcard. Each student holds their flashcard in     front of them with the picture side facing away.
      - Both students then take three steps away from each other before quickly   turning around
      - The two students then race to call out what's on the other student's     flashcard
      - The first student to correctly call out what's on the flashcard scores   a point for their team.
      - Play until all the flashcards have been used. The team with the most     points at the end of the game wins.

  • Online Games.

    • Fast Phrases



      This is a game for practicing English sentences.
      - Look at the images and make sentences to describe what you see.
      - To make the sentences, you have to choose from the options shown. If you click on the correct one, then that part of the sentence is added.
      - Points are awarded for correct hits and this multiplies if you continue to get the answers right.

    • Falling Clouds



      - You are presented with a jumbled sentence and have to rearrange the words to create an correct sentence.
      - The idea is that by re-ordering the words you can practice grammar points and also acquire vocabulary.
      ages appear at the top of the screen to provide meaning/context to the sentence.
      - If you can correctly arrange the clouds within a few moves, then you get a score bonus.

    • Bubbles



      - It is roughly based on pattern matching games in which gou have to match gems or colors.
      - In this game, rather than matching patterns of items, you click bubbles to make an English sentence.
      - The idea behind the game is that you can practice making grammatically correct phrases according to a visual stimulus. It is hoped that this can be a good way to practice grammar formations and also re-enforce vocabulary items.
      - To play, look at the image and think of the phrase that matches what you see. Then click on the bubbles to create a sentence that corresponds to the image.
      - You can either click each bubble or click the first and then drag the mouse over the rest.
      - If the sentence you create matches the image, then the bubbles will disappear. If you make a mistake while creating four sentence, then feedback and corrections will appear at the bottom.
      * For example, missing words will appear in red with an arrow pointing to where they should be.

Wrap up / Review

You can wrap up the lesson by playing a game
Distribute the flashcards around the classroom , then give the students simple instructions.

For example:
- Go to the library.
- Go to the park.
You can make the game more challenging by giving the students more complex instructions.
For example:
- Go to the library and then go to the park.
- Go to the park and then go to the library.
Even more challenging: You can Just use verbs
- Buy a book or eat a sandwich
- Go study or have a picnic
