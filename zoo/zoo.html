Introduction

Never rush in introducing a topic, Just slow down and elicit what they know about the topic

What noises do they make?
You may find that where you teach the animals make different noises to English ones! This is generally true of farm and domestic animals (cats, dogs, cows, ducks, etc) but see if any zoo animals make different noises. The children will love making the noises to compare to English zoo animal noises.

TPR

Reminder: TPR stands for Total Physical Response. It is a method of teaching that uses physical actions to help students learn.
It is a very effective method for teaching young learners.
But, Remember the students are going to imitate you. So: you have to be very clear and precise.
Be dramatic, but not over-dramatic. Be slow but not too slow

Some TPR You can use to teach Zoo:

  • Tiger: Act like a tiger. Roar, growl, and move your arms like a tiger.

  • Giraffe: Act like a giraffe. Stretch your neck and move your arms like a giraffe.

  • Monkey: Jump and put your hands under your armpits.

  • Penguin: Stick your arms to your sides with hands open to the outside and walk like a penguin.

  • Snake: Put your left hand under your right elbow and make your right hand's fingers look like a snake's head and do the hissing sound of a snake.

Games

How to explain a game or an activity to ESL Students

  • PPT Games

    • What can you see?  
      This game is included in the PPT flashcards
      It is basically A GOOGLE eyes or a telescope on click it will move to a part of the zoo animals flashcard let the students guess what animal is it.

    • Building a chain
       
      "It's a chain game ,first student will say the name of an animal and the next student will say the name of the animal previous student plus another animal and so on"
      For example: I see a tiger, I see a tiger and a giraffe, I see a tiger and a giraffe and a monkey, I see a tiger and a giraffe and a monkey and a penguin, and so on.

    • Match the animals game
      This game is two parts : Match the pictures and match the words.
      Click on "SHOW/HIDE" button to show the animals cards.Give the students 1 minute to memorize the cards. Then click on "SHOW/HIDE" button again to hide the cards.
      After that ask the students to come to the screen and click on the cards that match the animals.
      If the students click on the wrong card, Click the cards again to hide it.

  • In-Class Games

    • Animals Race

      Kids love this game, you can divide the class into teams then pick a student from each team, tell them a name of an animal and they have to race to the front of the class and act like the animal. The first team to finish wins.
      For example: "Monkey, go!" they will run to the front of the class and act like a monkey.

    • Zoo Animals Play-doh

      You'll need different colored play-doh. Sit down the students in groups and show them how to make simple models of animals. Let everyone make the animals that they want and as they are doing so circulate and ask lots of questions (e.g. What‚Äôs that? Is it a lion? What noise does a lion make? etc.).

    • Who Am I?

      Stick an animal picture onto each child‚Äôs back without them finding out what animal they are. To find out what animal they are, they will need to ask ‚Äúyes‚Äù or ‚Äúno‚Äù questions. e.g., ‚Äúdo I live in the forest‚Äù "How many legs do i have?"

    • Draw and Color

      For younger learners you can give them a coloring page and ask them to color the animal.
      For older learners you can give them a blank paper and ask them to draw a zoo animal.
      You can also ask them to write the name of the animal under the drawing.

  • Online Games.

    • Animals Mystery



      The target language is question sentences about animals such as 'Can it fly?' or 'How many legs does it have?'. Users can practice making a range of different question forms by clicking or tapping on word boxes. This is made into a game by having users ask questions about a hidden animal with a view to guessing which animal it is. When users appropriately form questions, they are given a simple response with an accompanying image to further clarify the meaning of the question and response.

    • Monster-vocab



      It's a multiple choice game, the students will have to choose the correct answer.
      The target language is vocabulary related to animals.
      You can mute the audio for older learners.
