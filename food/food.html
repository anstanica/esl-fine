"Reminder" at the beginning of every new topic, you have to elicit the vocabularies they know. You can do this by asking them or writing down the words,then asking them to point to the words they know. or showing the flashcards and asking them. Usually, you can elicit 5-10 words.

Introduction

You can introduce the new topic by showing the flashcards or The PPT slides. and explain the topic to them. for example, you can say "Today we are going to learn about food we eat daily". Start by asking them what they eat for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Then, you can ask them what they eat for snacks.And use famous food names, like pizza, burger, fries, etc.

Warm Up

Always a good warm up activity to get the students engaged and ready to learn is singing a song related to the topic. You can sing a song about food. Example "Do you like broccoli ice cream?".

Rules for teaching a song for the first time:

  • Let the kids watch the song first No interference required at this stage. So, they get an idea about the topic they are learning

  • Teach the song using TPR , Also use an audio version Convert to audio Here. Don't let the students watch a video while you are teaching them TPR / dance, they will easily be distracted

  • Repeat 2 OR 3 times and let the students repeat after you

  • Perform and sing together

Practice

Say the names out loud and ask students to repeat after you. repeat each food name 3 times.
Then, ask them to point to the food they eat. Example: "What do you eat for breakfast?".
A perfect approach is playing Pass & Say Hand a flashcard to one student and ask him to say and pass to the next etc... game

"Remember to correct their pronunciation, specially the first ones"

  • In-Class Games

    • The Whisperer

      "Students will sit or stand in a circle. lay the flashcards inside the circle.
        "if you don't have flash cards you can use a PPT file"
      and whisper the name of the food to the first student in the circle, the student will whisper the name to the next student and so on until the last student in the circle. . The last student will say the name of the food out loud and point to the card.
      The game continues until all the students have their turn in the game.

      Another approach:
      instead of you whispering the name of the food to the students, choose students to whisper the name of the food to the first student and so on .

      Also:
      "You can whisper the name of the food to the first student in the circle, the student will whisper the name to the next student and so on until the last student in the circle.
      The last student will say the name of the word out loud and the teacher will check if it is correct.
      IF it is correct, the student will keep the flashcard.
      IF the name is incorrect, the student will put the flashcard back in the circle and the game will continue.
      The student who has the most flashcards at the end of the game is the winner."

    • Memory Game

      Put the flashcards face down on a table or on the floor, after showing them to the students or you can cover them with blank papers, then ask the students "Where is the hamburger ?" OR "Which one is pizza ?"

    • Box of plastic food

      Get a box fill it with food toys ,ask the students put their hands inside the box and guess the name of the food. , and ask the student to show it to the class, if the student is correct, he will keep the food toy, and if he is incorrect, he will put it back

Wrap up

Wrap up the lesson by asking the students to tell you what they have learned in the lesson. For younger students, you can ask them to draw a picture of the food they have learned in the lesson. elicit the answers from the students, correct them if needed.
